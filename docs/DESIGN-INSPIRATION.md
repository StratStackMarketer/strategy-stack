## 1. Hero Section Designs

### Linear (linear.app)
**What to Reference:** Hero headline treatment + animated product UI
- **Visual:** "The product development system for teams and agents" - massive headline with animated interface elements showing real product features
- **Key Pattern:** Hero shows the ACTUAL product in motion, not stock imagery. Cards animate in, tasks update live, creates sense of the product "working"
- **Apply to Strategy Stack:** Show the AI/automation actually working in the hero - could be animated "reports generating" or "campaigns optimizing" in real-time

### Vercel (vercel.com)
**What to Reference:** Dark mode hero with gradient accents + stats credibility bar
- **Visual:** Deep black background, vibrant gradient text, clean monospace typography for technical credibility
- **Key Pattern:** Stats ribbon ("99.999% uptime", "$1.4T processed") immediately after hero establishes credibility
- **Apply to Strategy Stack:** Consider stats bar after hero: "500+ campaigns launched", "47% average ROI increase", etc.

### basement.studio (basement.studio)
**What to Reference:** Agency attitude in headline + client logo ticker
- **Visual:** "We make cool shit that performs" - unapologetic, memorable headline. Horizontal scrolling client logos (Vercel, Linear, Cursor, MrBeast)
- **Key Pattern:** Combines attitude with credibility. Bold claim backed by recognizable logos
- **Apply to Strategy Stack:** Could adapt: "AI-powered marketing that actually works." followed by client/result logos

### Raycast (raycast.com)
**What to Reference:** Keyboard-centric animation + feature highlights
- **Visual:** Animated keyboard with feature callouts appearing on different keys. Shows product through metaphor rather than screenshot
- **Key Pattern:** Creative visual metaphor that's interactive and memorable
- **Apply to Strategy Stack:** Could show "marketing dashboard" elements revealing features on hover/scroll

### Common Brutalist Patterns to Adapt
1. **System fonts** (or deliberately "ugly" fonts) - Strategy Stack could use Inter or a mono font for certain elements
2. **Raw borders** - 1-2px solid black borders instead of soft shadows
3. **Oversized text** - Headlines that dominate the viewport
4. **Minimal color palette** - Usually 2-3 colors max
5. **Grid visibility** - Let the layout grid show through

## 8. Animation Pattern Reference

### Page Load Animations
- **Staggered fade-in:** Elements appear sequentially (50-100ms delay each)
- **Text reveal:** Headlines slide up from below with clip mask
- **Scale + fade:** Elements start at 95% scale and grow to 100%

### Scroll-Triggered Animations
- **Parallax sections:** Background moves slower than foreground
- **Progressive reveal:** Stats count up as section enters viewport
- **Sticky elements:** Hero elements pin during scroll, new content slides over

### Hover Micro-interactions
- **Button transforms:** Scale 1.02-1.05 + slight Y-translation (-2px)
- **Link underlines:** Animated underline draws on hover
- **Card lifts:** translateY(-4px) + shadow increase
- **Color shifts:** Background/accent color transition (200-300ms ease)

### Loading States
- **Skeleton screens:** Gray placeholder boxes that pulse
- **Progressive image loading:** Blur-up effect from low-res placeholder
- **Spinner alternatives:** Progress bars, animated icons

## 10. Color & Typography Notes

### From Research - Common Patterns

**Dark Mode Hero Sites:**
- Background: #0A0A0B to #18181B
- Accent: Electric blue (#3B82F6), Vibrant purple (#8B5CF6), or Lime (#84CC16)
- Text: Pure white (#FFFFFF) for headlines, muted (#A1A1AA) for body

**Typography Stacks Observed:**
- Linear: Inter (clean, modern)
- Vercel: Geist (proprietary, mono elements)
- Raycast: SF Pro (Apple system)
- Brutalist sites: System-ui, monospace accents

**Recommended for Strategy Stack:**
- Headlines: Bold sans-serif (Inter Black, or custom)
- Body: Regular weight for readability
- Accents: Monospace for stats/numbers
- Primary: Deep black + electric accent
- Consider: Lime green accent for "growth" messaging
